About Jane Wentzien

30 years of clinical expertise in physical therapy and rehabilitation

Clinical Background

Jane Wentzien is a senior physical therapist with over three decades of hands-on clinical experience spanning acute care, outpatient rehabilitation, and long-term recovery management.

Her career has been defined by a commitment to understanding not just the protocols, but the human realities of recovery—the psychological barriers, the lifestyle constraints, and the individual variations that determine whether a treatment plan succeeds or fails in the real world.

For the past decade, Jane has led rehabilitation teams, training clinicians to recognize the subtle signs that standardized approaches miss. This experience revealed a critical gap: as healthcare scales through technology, the nuanced clinical judgment that prevents failures often gets lost.

Today, Jane applies this senior clinical perspective to help organizations and individuals avoid the blind spots that undermine rehabilitation outcomes at scale.

Philosophy

Recovery is not linear, and people are not protocols. Every successful rehabilitation program must account for the unpredictable nature of human healing—pain responses vary, motivation fluctuates, and life circumstances intervene.

Jane's approach centers on identifying where assumptions break down. Whether reviewing a digital health platform serving thousands or creating a personalized plan for an individual, the goal is the same: anticipate the failure points before they become failures.

This is not about validating outcomes or certifying quality. It's about applying decades of clinical judgment to surface the risks that data alone cannot reveal.
